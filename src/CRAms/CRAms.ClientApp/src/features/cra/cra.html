Cyber Resilience Act

Overview

  • Establishment of mandatory standard measures for retailers, manufacturers, and importers regarding the cybersecurity of Products with digital Elements
  • Measures apply to Products with digital Elements that are placed on the EU market
  • Products with digital Elements include any software or hardware products, as well as any solutions involved in processing data from these products over a network
  • Software as a Service (SaaS) products which are purely available as a service that do not require downloading or installing and are not essential for the functioning of a connected hardware product as well as non-commercial Open Source Software are not Products with digital Elements
  • Risks related to the cybersecurity of products with digital Elements must be taken into account as early as the design phase
  • Products with digital Elements may only be marketed in the EU if they do not have any known vulnerabilities
  • Requirements are placed on the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of Products with digital Elements
  • In addition to the requirement to minimize data, users must be able to easily delete their data
  • Appropriate strategies and procedures for vulnerability management must be implemented
  • Identified vulnerabilities must be addressed and made available to users as soon as possible
  • (Third-party) Components used in the product must be documented in a Software Bill of Materials (SBOM)
